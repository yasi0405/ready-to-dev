An MVP is not a poor version of the final product. It is a learning instrument meant to reduce business and technical uncertainty.

## Frame the main hypothesis first

Before building, define which risk must fall first: desirability, feasibility, distribution or economics.

## Choose a technically sound core

The MVP should stay light, but not disposable. A healthy foundation avoids painful rewrites exactly when product traction starts to appear.

## Instrument useful signals

Without telemetry, event tracking and qualitative feedback tied to usage, an MVP tends to generate noise instead of knowledge.
